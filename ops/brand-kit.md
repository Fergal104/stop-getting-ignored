# Stop Getting Ignored Brand Kit

## Positioning

- Brand: `Stop Getting Ignored`
- Promise: Practical outreach systems for solo operators who need replies, not motivation.
- Market: solo freelancers and tiny agencies selling services worth $500 to $5,000.
- Core problem: their outreach gets ignored because the message is generic, high-friction, or badly timed.
- Core outcome: more replies from better-fit prospects without writing every message from scratch.

## Customer Snapshot

- Works solo or with one to three collaborators.
- Sells copy, design, development, automation, email, content, or growth services.
- Has some proof of skill but no reliable outbound system.
- Wants revenue quickly and does not want to become a sales-content creator.

## Messaging Angles

- Bad outreach is usually a positioning problem in disguise.
- Specific observation beats generic flattery.
- Low-friction asks beat aggressive meeting requests.
- Follow-up quality matters as much as the first message.

## Tone Guide

Use:
- direct
- practical
- observant
- low-ego
- skeptical of hype

Avoid:
- fake urgency
- broad income claims
- manipulative language
- overpromising conversion lifts
- guru phrasing like `secret`, `hack`, or `crush it`
